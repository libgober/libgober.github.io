Brian Libgober is a political scientist and legal scholar. His research focuses on the political economy of American institutions, with a special emphasis on the making of regulations by executive agencies. Thematically, he is interested in the relationship between economic inequality, interest group power, and the design of legal institutions. Methodologically, his work combines a variety of approaches, including formal models, quantitative empirics, and case studies.

Brian Libgober is an Assistant Professor of Political Science and Law at Northwestern University, and a Faculty Fellow at the Institute for Policy Research. He is also a Senior Research Scholar at Yale Law School. Previously, Brian was an Assistant Professor at the University of California, San Diego and a postdoctoral fellow in Political Science at Yale.

His research has been published in the Journal of Politics, the Quarterly Journal of Political Science, Political Analysis, the Journal of Public Administration Research & Theory, and other peer-reviewed journals, and has received financial support from the National Science Foundation, the Russell Sage Foundation, the Washington Center for Equitable Growth, and others.
